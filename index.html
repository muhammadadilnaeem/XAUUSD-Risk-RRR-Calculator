Formula & Calculation Guide
📏
Risk Points & Reward Points
Distance from entry to SL / TP in price units
Risk Points = |Entry − Stop Loss| Reward Points = |Take Profit − Entry|
Absolute value is used so the formula works for both LONG and SHORT trades. For LONG: Entry > SL and TP > Entry. For SHORT: Entry < SL and TP < Entry.
VariableMeaning
EntryYour trade open price (e.g. 2000.00)
SLStop Loss — where you exit if the trade moves against you
TPTake Profit — your target exit where you lock in profit
💵
Risk in Dollars & Reward in Dollars
Monetary value of your risk and potential gain
Risk ($) = Risk Points × Lot Size × 100 Reward ($) = Reward Points × Lot Size × 100
For XAUUSD, 1 standard lot = 100 oz of gold. A $1.00 price move on 1 standard lot = $100 P&L. So the multiplier is Lot Size × 100, giving dollar value per point.
VariableMeaning
Lot SizeNumber of lots traded (0.01 = micro, 0.10 = mini, 1.00 = standard)
× 100XAUUSD contract = 100 oz/lot → $100 per full point per standard lot
Example: 5 risk points × 0.01 lot × 100 = $5.00 at risk. The same move on 0.10 lot = $50 at risk.
📊
Risk Percentage
Portion of your account balance being risked
Risk (%) = ( Risk ($) ÷ Account Balance ) × 100
This tells you what fraction of your trading capital is at stake on this single trade. Professional traders treat this as the single most important number — it governs long-term survival.
VariableMeaning
Risk ($)Dollar loss if SL is hit (calculated from formula above)
BalanceYour current account equity in USD
⚖️
Risk-to-Reward Ratio (RRR)
How much you gain vs. how much you risk per trade
RRR = Reward ($) ÷ Risk ($) → expressed as 1 : RRR
A RRR of 2.0 means "for every $1 risked, you stand to make $2." Always expressed with Risk as 1, so RRR = 2.5 is written as 1 : 2.5.
Why it matters: With 1:2 RRR you only need to be right 34% of the time to break even. With 1:3 that drops to 25%. Higher RRR = more room to be wrong.
📐

How much a $1 price move is worth at your lot size
$ per Point = Lot Size × 100
Gold is quoted in USD per troy ounce. 1 standard lot = 100 oz, so a $1 price change = $100 P&L. Scale linearly with lot size.

Reverse-Engineering Lot Size
Find the correct lot size for a fixed risk amount
Max Risk ($) = Account Balance × (Risk % ÷ 100) Lot Size = Max Risk ($) ÷ (Risk Points × 100)
Use this to position-size correctly. Decide your risk % first, then solve for lot size — never the other way around. This keeps risk consistent regardless of SL distance.
Example: $5,000 balance · 1% risk · 15-point SL
Max Risk = $5,000 × 0.01 = $50
Lot Size = $50 ÷ (15 × 100) = 0.033 lots
💱
Points vs Pips in XAUUSD
Terminology used throughout this calculator
1 Point = $1.00 price change in XAUUSD (e.g. 2000.00 → 2001.00 = 1 point) 1 Pip = 0.01 price change (2nd decimal place) (e.g. 2000.00 → 2000.01 = 1 pip = 0.01 points)
This calculator uses points (whole dollar moves) as the base unit since SL and TP are typically set in whole increments for gold. Some brokers display gold in "pips" (0.01) — divide by 100 to convert to points.

Full Worked Example
Step-by-step using the default values in this calculator
Given: Account Balance = $1,000.00 Lot Size = 0.01 Entry Price = 2000.00 Stop Loss (SL) = 1995.00 Take Profit (TP) = 2010.00
1️⃣
Step 1 — Calculate Points
Risk Points = |2000.00 − 1995.00| = 5.00 pts Reward Points = |2010.00 − 2000.00| = 10.00 pts
2️⃣
Step 2 — Dollar Value per Point
$ per Point = 0.01 lot × 100 = $1.00 per point
3️⃣
Step 3 — Risk & Reward in Dollars
Risk ($) = 5.00 pts × $1.00/pt = $5.00 Reward ($) = 10.00 pts × $1.00/pt = $10.00
4️⃣
Step 4 — Risk Percentage
Risk (%) = ($5.00 ÷ $1,000.00) × 100 = 0.50%
✅ 0.50% — within the professional safe zone (below 1%)
5️⃣
Step 5 — Risk-to-Reward Ratio
RRR = $10.00 ÷ $5.00 = 2.00 → 1 : 2.00
✅ 1:2 — meets the professional minimum (good trade)
Final verdict: This trade risks $5 to make $10 (1:2 RRR), using only 0.50% of the $1,000 account. Break-even win rate = 33.3%. This is a well-structured, low-risk trade that aligns with professional standards.
